A SUMMARY OF THE FULHAM AREA, LONDON

Situated south west of London in the Borough of Hammersmith and Fulham and only a few miles from the city centre. Near the River Thames, the area is a vibrant and fun place filled bars and restaurants and you will also find the famous Fulham Football Ground, Craven Cottage in the vicinity, thoroughly sadly they have been relegated and are not playing so well at the moment.

Not quite Chelsea, but is very popular with young professionals and backpackers alike, making it one of London`s most expensive suburbs and and area to live and be seen in, loved by locals and visitors to the area.

RELEVANT POSTCODES FOR FULHAM AND HAMMERSMITH DISTRICT INCLUDE SW6, SW10, W3, W4, W6, W110, W11, W12 AND W14

THINGS TO DO IN THE FULHAM AREA

SIGHTSEEING

Fulham Palace - Bishops Avenue, London, SW6 6EA - Telephone: +44(0) 20 7736 3233 Dating back to the 11th century, the Palace was home to the Bishops of London until 1973. In modern times the estate covers 12 acres and boasts the longest moat in England. The Palace consists of a Museum, Art Gallery and Grounds and makes a great day out for all the family.

Opening hours:

Museum: Mon-Thur 12.30-4.30pm, Sun & Bank holiday Mondays noon-5pm (summer)

Mon-Thur 12.30-3.30pm, Sun & Bank holiday Mondays noon-4pm (winter)

Gardens open daily dusk till dawn.
